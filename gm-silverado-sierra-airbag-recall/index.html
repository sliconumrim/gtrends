gm silverado sierra airbag recall

In a move aimed at ensuring the safety of its customers, General Motors has issued a recall for certain units of its Chevrolet Silverado and GMC Sierra pickup trucks. The recall affects vehicles from 2018-2019 model years and is in response to a defect that could potentially lead to injury or a crash. The issue lies with the roof-rail airbag (RRAB) inflators on affected models, which may experience a sidewall rupture or have their end cap detach when deployed. If this occurs, it could allow compressed gas to rapidly escape, propelling fragments of the airbag canister into the cabin and posing a risk to occupants. General Motors has identified the following vehicles as being part of this recall: 2018 GMC Sierra 1500 2019 GMC Sierra 2500 and 3500 2018 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 Crew Cab 2018 and 2019 Chevrolet Silverado 2500 and 3500 Owners of these vehicles will be notified by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) or by their local dealership. Affected owners should contact their local GM dealership to schedule a free repair, which involves replacing the RRAB inflator module with a new one that addresses this safety issue. By taking proactive steps like this recall, General Motors is demonstrating its commitment to customer safety and well-being.
